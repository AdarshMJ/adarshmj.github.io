Maybe something can happen May...

Adarsh Jamadandi, May 10, 2022

A crisp day hangs tight between the sepia strewn dullard of winter night…

I am no longer woken up by the warm sunrise hitting my face..

I clutch my linty mattress like a tottering crutch…

As I struggle to stitch myself up from stale air and dull headaches smeared across my walls.

If you look closely at the cracks, you can see the rifts trickling with emptiness…

As I collect myself I am careful not to spill my guts…

Because you’d then see the emptiness just walking out of the room…”

A Man without Love by Engelbert Humperdinck is playing on Youtube. The song is roughly 3minutes 17seconds long. Well, it started out with Hurt by Johnny Cash. Somebody has synced the song with Hugh Jackman's Logan. It fits perfectly. The visual and the lyrics. Or at least it seems to me that way. I am on my last piece of gummy bear.


The next song that I deliberately search for is A Man without Love by Engelbert Humperdinck, as I said it is 3 minutes and 17 seconds long. After Mr. Humperdinck has finished exalting, how he wakes up everyday and breaks up every day because a man is lonely without love…. Youtube decides it should play A Man without love by Engelbert Humperdinck… again… but this time somebody has put it in a loop and it is 1hour 02 minutes and 32 seconds long. I have already listened to it for 20 minutes. You need to play it on a loop so that the lyrics start making sense. Like syncing Johnny Cash's Hurt to Logan. It should be in synchronisation. I let it play. Its still playing…


On another of my browser tab, is a code running. I do that sometimes. Run code. If you want to know the technical details, it is a graph neural network that is running, working on some graph dataset. To ensure it doesn't stop running, I turn on Amphetamine, it prevents my laptop from going to sleep. I also shove the charging cable, just so that it doesn't die out, I do this, while I am pouring myself a cup of coffee. Black. Maybe with a tinge of honey. Black coffee sucks. But I drink it anyway.


A Man without love by Engelbert Humperdinck is at 29:26 now. I go back to check the progress of my code, it says 50 Epochs and the M.A.E is at 3.66… which is essentially shit. The M.A.E has to be lower. But I suspect it might get there, I am letting it run for 300 epochs. But maybe not. Maybe it is bored. Of the routine.


While I check the progress of the code, I mentally try to ascertain where Mr. Humperdinck is at - he's voice crescendoes into - "Every day I start out, then I cry my heart out…."

Cool!.. Another cycle of song and another epoch too. All this, while I pour myself a bowl of cereal and some low-fat milk, as I sit down with my bowl, a fistful of sorrow passes my ribcage. I quickly stuff my mouth with cereal, the cereal is really bland and it sometimes hurts the inside of my mouth because the cereal isnt soft enough. And I mainly attribute this to the low-fat milk and the Plath-istic neurotic rot that manages to settle on my work desk and my bedsheets. I pay no attention to the sorrow. It passes. Oh sorry, I had paused the music. It is still at 38:49. Stuck. Forever. In time. Held in its place by a 60W charger shoved into its crevice and amphetamine.


I am distracted now. I fire up my kindle and get back to reading Normal People by Sally Rooney, I think I mainly bought the book because of Daisy Edgar Jones. I can now put a face to Marianne. I don't know who plays Connell, but I remember what he thinks -


"She looked like a piece of religious art. It was so much more painful to look at her than anyone had warned him it would be, and he wanted to do something terrible, like set himself on fire or drive his car into a tree. He always reflexively imagined ways to cause himself extreme injury when he was distressed. It seemed to soothe him briefly, the act of imagining a much worse and more totalising pain than the one he really felt, maybe just the cognitive energy it required, the momentary break in his train of thought, but afterwards he would only feel worse."


The M.A.E is at 2.56 which is better. Not the best of the performance, but better. Maybe I should experiment with a higher hidden size for the network. But then, it means the laptop has to stay up much longer. More amphetamine. More coffee. Black. No honey this time.


A Man without love by Engelbert Humperdinck is now at 50:00…But Colab does not seem to co-operate, it refuses to connect.

Maybe it is tired of our dreary dysfunctional rendezvous…I keep apologising. Now who am I gonna offend?

There is just too much of rot everywhere… the kind of rot that pretty boys don't pair with their favourite denim jackets.
